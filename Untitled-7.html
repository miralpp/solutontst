Motivation

Projectile motion, while simple in appearance, offers a deep dive into the fundamental principles of physics. It allows us to explore a versatile framework governed by both linear and quadratic equations. Parameters like initial velocity, gravitational acceleration, and launch height provide opportunities to model real-world phenomena such as the trajectory of a soccer ball or a rocket.

Theoretical Foundation

Projectile motion follows the governing equations derived from Newtonâ€™s laws:

  • Horizontal Motion: \(x(t) = v_0 \cdot \cos(\theta) \cdot t\)
  • Vertical Motion: \(y(t) = v_0 \cdot \sin(\theta) \cdot t - \frac{1}{2} g \cdot t^2\)

Where:

  • \(v_0\): Initial velocity
  • \(\theta\): Angle of projection
  • \(g\): Gravitational acceleration
  • \(t\): Time

Range Analysis

The horizontal range (\(R\)) is given by:

\( R = \frac{{v_0^2 \cdot \sin(2\theta)}}{g} \)

Key insights:

  • Maximum range occurs at \(45^\circ\) in ideal conditions (flat terrain, no air resistance).
  • Variations in initial velocity (\(v_0\)) and gravitational acceleration (\(g\)) significantly affect the range.

Practical Applications

This model is adaptable to various real-world scenarios:

  • Sports: Calculating the arc of a soccer ball.
  • Engineering: Optimizing rocket launch trajectories.
  • Uneven terrain: Accounting for initial height (\(h\)).

Simulation and Code

Below is a Python script to simulate projectile motion:

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def projectile_motion(v0, theta, g=9.81, h=0):
    theta_rad = np.radians(theta)
    t_flight = (v0 * np.sin(theta_rad) + np.sqrt((v0 * np.sin(theta_rad))**2 + 2 * g * h)) / g
    t = np.linspace(0, t_flight, num=500)
    x = v0 * np.cos(theta_rad) * t
    y = v0 * np.sin(theta_rad) * t - 0.5 * g * t**2
    return x, y

v0 = 20  # Initial velocity (m/s)
theta = 45  # Projection angle (degrees)
x, y = projectile_motion(v0, theta)

plt.plot(x, y)
plt.title("Projectile Motion")
plt.xlabel("Horizontal Distance (m)")
plt.ylabel("Vertical Distance (m)")
plt.grid()
plt.show()
